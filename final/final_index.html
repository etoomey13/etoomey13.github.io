Welcome to Best in the Burgh!

Whether you're a native yinzer or a vistor trying to decode the local's Pittsburghese, this is your all-inclusive guide to seeing the best that Pittsburgh has to offer!

To start, let's get to know more about the city:

  1. Pittsburgh's most popular nickname, "Steel City," came about due to its booming coal and steel industries in the 1900s.
  2. Pittsburgh has more bridges than any other city in the world at 446.
  3. Pittsburgher's bleed black and gold. It is the only city in which all of the professional sports teams (Penguins, Pirates, and Steelers) deem black and gold as their colors.
  4. Pittsburgh was and still is a forerunner in medicine. In 1954, Dr. Jonas Salk developed the polio vaccine while conducting research at the University of Pittsburgh.
  5. Pittsburgh is the proud home of Heinz Ketchup, created by Henry Heinz in the 1860s.
  6. Pittsburgher's have their own dialect called Pittsburghese. It takes some getting used to, so here's a crash course to help you out:
